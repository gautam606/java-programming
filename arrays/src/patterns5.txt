Step 1: What is an Interval?

An interval is simply a range.

For example:

[1,3]

means

1 2 3

Another interval:

[5,8]

means

5 6 7 8

So an interval has:

Start
End
[ Start , End ]
Step 2: What does "Merge" mean?

Merge means combine two intervals if they overlap.

Imagine two people booking a meeting room.

Person A booked

1 PM ----- 3 PM

Person B booked

2 PM ----- 5 PM

Visualize it:

1-----3
   2---------5

They overlap.

Instead of storing

[1,3]
[2,5]

we can simply store

[1,5]

This is called merging.

Step 3: When do intervals overlap?

Suppose

[1,4]
[2,6]

Draw them.

1---------4
   2-----------6

They share common numbers.

2
3
4

So they overlap.

Merged interval

[1,6]

Another example

[1,3]
[5,7]

Draw

1------3

          5------7

No touching.

No overlap.

Keep both.

[1,3]
[5,7]

The Golden Rule (Most Important)

Suppose:

Current Interval = [start1, end1]

Next Interval = [start2, end2]

Ask:

Is start2 <= end1 ?
Yes → Overlap → Merge.
No → No overlap → Add current interval to the result and move to the next.
